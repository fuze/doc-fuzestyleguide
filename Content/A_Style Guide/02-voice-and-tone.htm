Voice and Tone

(Submit comments here)

One way we write empowering content is by being aware of our voice and our tone. This section explains the difference between voice and tone, and lays out the elements of each as they apply to Fuze.

What’s the difference between voice and tone? Think of it this way: You have the same voice all the time, but your tone changes. You might use one tone when you're out to dinner with your closest friends, and a different tone when you're in a meeting with your boss.

Your tone also changes depending on the emotional state of the person you’re addressing. You wouldn’t want to use the same tone of voice with someone who’s scared or upset as you would with someone who’s laughing.

The same is true for Fuze. Our voice doesn’t change much from day to day, but our tone changes all the time.

Voice

Fuze’s voice is human. It’s familiar, friendly, and straightforward. Our priority is explaining our products and helping our users get their work done so they can get on with their lives. We want to educate people without patronizing or confusing them.

One way to think of our voice is to compare what it is to what it isn’t. Fuze’s voice is:

  • Modern but not futuristic
  • smart but not aloof
  • cool but friendly
  • human and flexible
  • ambitious
  • passionate
  • forward-looking
  • agile and innovative

Tone

Fuze’s tone is usually informal, but it’s always more important to be clear than entertaining. When you’re writing, consider the reader’s state of mind. Are they new to our products? Are they confused and new to VoIP in general? Are they curious about a post on our blog? Once you have an idea of their emotional state, you can adjust your tone accordingly.

Fuze has a sense of humor, so feel free to be funny when it’s appropriate and when it comes naturally to you. But don’t go out of your way to make a joke—forced humor can be worse than none at all. If you’re unsure, keep a straight face.

Style tips

Here are a few key elements of writing Fuze’s voice. For more, see the Grammar and mechanics section.

  • Use active voice Avoid passive voice (i.e. the subject of the sentence does the action)
  • Use context to reduce ambiguity (i.e. structure your thoughts)
  • Use positive language Use positive language rather than negative language. (e.g. “To get a donut, stand in line.” As opposed to “You can’t get a donut if you don’t stand in line”)

Fuze Bot is Fuze's mascot. He captures the spirit of our brand’s personality. He smiles, winks, and sometimes high-fives, but he does not talk. Don't write in his voice.
